ME210 COMPETITION

Electrical

Electrical Requirements

  • Operates as a stand-alone entity, operating completely untethered
  • Power must be supplied by the provided batteries only (two 7.2V NiMH rechargeable battery packs)
  • Each DROID should incorporate an easily accessible toggle switch on its exterior, which will serve as an emergency stop switch. The switch must cut all power to the DROID when toggled.
  • A fuse must be installed.

System Overview

We used an Arduino Mega to control the drivetrain and sensing since a single Arduino Uno could not afford enough pins and simultaneously controlling multiple Arduino Unos proved unnecessarily difficult.

Power

We used two 7.2V NiMH rechargeable battery packs connected in series. This was then connected to a 5A fuse and a switch to turn on the entire robot, and then to 2 voltage step-down regulators (LM2596S) which outputted 7.7V to the Arduino Mega and 13.4V to the motor drivers (since the L298 motor drivers have a 1V drop internally and we needed to run the motors at 12V).

Motor Drivers

We used two L298 dual-full bridge drivers to drive our motors for the wheels and another L298 to drive the flywheel motor. These were controlled by PWM pulses from the Arduino Mega. For the puck reloader stepper motor, we used a Nema17 stepper motor and the DRV8825 stepper driver.

IR Line Detection

Our robot detects the black line on the ground through a conditioning circuit that filters out sunlight (DC) with a high-pass filter and uses Schmitt trigger hysteresis to account for noise.

IR Beacon Sensing

Our robot detects the IR beacons by rotating and detecting a spike in the voltage input, which represents the signal from the IR beacon. We built a filtering circuit that includes an amplifier, a bandpass filter to remove extraneous frequencies with a peak frequency at 909Hz, and a Schmitt trigger to account for noise in the signal. For the bandpass filter, we chose an active narrow bandpass which limits the passband to 45.45Hz.

Through testing, we set the threshold amplitude to 2.1V. If the signal read by the Arduino pin exceeds 2.1V, the robot knows it is facing the direction of an IR beacon.
